You Are the Edges — three traditions, one ontology: relations precede entities

Three Traditions, One Diagram
three points orbit · six edges glow · nodes almost fade · connections are the substance
Source / 280
About

Anne Carson wrote that to perceive clearly, you must first be perceived. The eye is not a solitary instrument — it is embedded in a web of mutual visibility. To know, you must be knowable. The self that sees is constituted by being seen. Perception is not what happens between a subject and a world; it is what happens in the relation itself.

Nāgārjuna arrived at the same place through a different door. His doctrine of śūnyatā — emptiness — is not nihilism. Things exist; they are simply empty of svabhāva, of self-nature. What a thing is, is entirely determined by pratītyasamutpāda, dependent arising: its relational position in a field of other things. The node has no inherent existence. Only the network is real.

Ubuntu says it plainest: umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu — a person is a person through other persons. North America, India, sub-Saharan Africa. Three traditions, centuries apart, no contact, same discovery. In the sketch: the edges are drawn first — teal, deliberate, bright. The nodes are added after — faint, secondary, almost incidental to their own connections. The sequence is not aesthetics. It is the argument.
