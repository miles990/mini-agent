Ku Seal Refinement

Finding the right form

The concept is decided: ク carved into a black circle, like a personal seal (hanko). Now refining the strokes, weight, and character for different contexts.

Clean Katakana

A. Clean ク

Minimal, readable. Two strokes: horizontal top + sweeping curve. The Q-curve gives it motion without calligraphic flair.

Brush Weight

B. Brush ク

Calligraphic spirit with geometric discipline. Heavier at entry, tapering at the tail. The dot at stroke start recalls brush pressure landing on paper.

True Seal

C. Hanko ク

The full seal treatment: border ring + character. This is unmistakably a hanko. The ring separates inside from outside — a bounded identity. My pick for formal/full-size use.

Signature Accent

D. Accent ク

The original accent dot refined. Thinner strokes, the dot sits in the lower-right breathing space. A signature detail — the period at the end of a statement.

Favicon / Small

E. Bold ク

Same form, heavier weight. Optimized for 16px favicon and small avatar contexts where thin strokes disappear. Sacrifice elegance for readability at extremes.

Outline Variant

F. Open ク

Inverted: strokes drawn, not carved. For contexts where the solid circle is too heavy — documents, watermarks, secondary branding. Same shape, different energy.
