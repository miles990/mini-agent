## 1. Cursor (Anysphere)

### The 30-Second Pitch
"An AI-native code editor that feels like VS Code but writes code for you. Type what you want in plain English, get working code instantly."

### First 1,000 Users
- Four MIT founders (Michael Truell, Sualeh Asif, Arvid Lunnemark, Aman Sanger) shipped a public beta within **3 months** of first line of code (May 2023).
- The team initially "evangelized" on social networks to build an early waitlist. Truell said: "That was helpful getting us kick-started. But then after that, we kind of stepped away from that."
- Forking VS Code was the critical decision: zero learning curve, existing plugins work, developers could switch in minutes.
- First users came from Twitter/X developer circles and Hacker News. The team actively engaged on both platforms, treating early adopters as collaborators.

### Growth Trajectory
| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Public beta | May 2023 |
| ~$1M ARR | End of 2023 |
| ~$4M ARR | Spring 2024 |
| ~$48M ARR | October 2024 |
| $100M ARR | ~12 months post-launch |
| 1M users / 360K paying | ~16 months post-launch |
| $500M ARR | ~Month 30 |
| $1.2B ARR | End of 2025 |
| 1B lines of code generated daily | 2025 |

### Distribution Channels
- **$0 spent on marketing.** All growth was organic.
- **Twitter/X**: Primary discovery channel. Developers sharing "wow" moments and productivity gains.
- **Hacker News**: Team actively responded; multiple front-page threads.
- **Product Hunt**: Launched 5 times. 2024 Product of the Year. Consistently Top 5 Products of the Day.
- **Lex Fridman Podcast**: CEO appeared, articulating vision of AI-assisted coding to a massive technical audience.
- **Discord & Forums**: Community of developer evangelists sharing tips, workflows, and snippets.
- **YouTube**: User-generated content (250-minute guides, tutorials) became free onboarding material.

### What Made People Share It (Viral Mechanics)
1. **The "wow moment"**: Typing plain English and watching working code appear was genuinely magical. Users couldn't help but tell others.
2. **Andrej Karpathy's "Vibe Coding" tweet** (Feb 2025): Viewed 4.5M+ times. Described "fully giving in to the vibes" using Cursor + Claude. The term became Collins Dictionary Word of the Year for 2025. This was Cursor's single biggest viral accelerant.
3. **Viral stories**: An 8-year-old building a game in Cursor. Voice-input coding demos. These stories traveled organically.
4. **Developer tool optics**: When a senior engineer switches editors, everyone notices. "In developer tools, word-of-mouth dynamics are unusually potent."
5. **Cool-kid customers**: When OpenAI, Midjourney, Perplexity, Shopify are visibly using your tool, it creates aspirational adoption.

### Pricing Strategy & Its Role in Adoption
- **Free tier**: 2,000 completions/month. Genuinely useful, not crippled. "A free tier that feels generous creates evangelists."
- **Pro**: $20/month
- **Business**: $40/month
- **Usage-based agent pricing**: Added August 2025
- **36% free-to-paid conversion rate** (vs. typical 2-8% for SaaS freemium). The product was so good that a third of free users paid.
- OpenAI's Startup Fund invested early, adding credibility. OpenAI reportedly attempted acquisition in 2024.

### Key Insight
Cursor proves that in developer tools, a genuinely superior product IS the marketing. They built a self-reinforcing loop: great product --> developer shares it --> more users --> more data --> better product.

## 2. Devin (Cognition)

### The 30-Second Pitch
"The first AI software engineer. Give Devin a task in plain English and it autonomously plans, writes code, debugs, and deploys -- like hiring a junior developer that works 24/7."

### First 1,000 Users
- Cognition operated in **stealth** until March 2024.
- Launch strategy was a single, carefully choreographed demo video posted to X/Twitter.
- The demo showed Devin autonomously fixing a bug in an open-source library and completing tasks on Upwork.
- **The video hit 30M+ views on X.** Instant virality.
- Early access was invite-only and waitlist-gated, creating scarcity and exclusivity.

### Growth Trajectory
| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Founded | November 2023 |
| Viral launch demo | March 2024 |
| General availability | December 2024 (at $500/mo) |
| $1M ARR | September 2024 |
| $73M ARR | June 2025 |
| Acquired Windsurf | July 2025 |
| Combined ~$150M ARR | Post-acquisition |
| $400M Series C at $10.2B | September 2025 |

### Distribution Channels
- **Twitter/X**: THE launch channel. The viral demo was the entire initial marketing strategy.
- **Tech press**: Massive coverage from the "first AI software engineer" framing.
- **Benchmark marketing**: Touted 13.9% on SWE-bench vs. GPT-4's 1.7%, giving the tech press a concrete headline.
- **Controversy as fuel**: The debunking videos (Internet of Bugs, etc.) generated as much awareness as the original demo.
- **CEO endorsements**: Ramp CEO called it "the single most impressive demo of the prior decade."

### What Made People Share It (Viral Mechanics)
1. **Fear and awe framing**: "Will AI replace software engineers?" The demo triggered an emotional response that demanded sharing.
2. **Controversy loop**: Demo --> debunking videos --> counter-arguments --> more coverage. The controversy kept Devin in the conversation for months.
3. **Bold claim**: "First AI software engineer" was deliberately provocative, designed to spark debate.
4. **Aspirational for non-technical audiences**: Executives and managers shared it imagining replacing engineering headcount.

### The Controversy (Critical Marketing Detail)
- YouTube channel "Internet of Bugs" exposed cherry-picked examples, self-inflicted bugs Devin "fixed," and mismatched deliverables.
- Answer.AI tested Devin: only 3 out of 20 tasks completed successfully (15% success rate).
- The Register headline: "'First AI software engineer' is bad at its job."
- **Paradox**: The controversy hurt credibility with senior engineers but boosted brand awareness enormously. More people know about Devin than any competitor except Cursor/Copilot.

### Pricing Strategy & Its Role in Adoption
- **Initial**: $500/month for teams (December 2024). High price signaled enterprise positioning but limited individual adoption.
- **Devin 2.0**: Slashed to $20/month Core plan + pay-as-you-go ($2.25/ACU). Dramatically expanded addressable market to individual developers, students, and small businesses.
- The price cut was a strategic admission that $500/mo was too high a barrier for the bottom-up adoption that drives developer tools.

### Key Insight
Devin's story is a masterclass in **hype-driven launch marketing** with a cautionary tale. The viral demo created explosive awareness but overpromised, leading to backlash. Despite this, revenue grew 73x in 9 months. Lesson: in AI, controversy is awareness, and awareness converts -- even imperfect products find users if the vision is compelling.

## 3. Windsurf (Codeium)

### The 30-Second Pitch
"An AI-first IDE where AI is your coding collaborator, not just a suggestion engine. Cascade reads your entire codebase, proposes multi-file changes, and runs commands -- all from a single prompt."

### First 1,000 Users
- **Not a cold start.** Codeium had built an 800,000-user base with its free autocomplete extension before launching Windsurf.
- Windsurf Editor launched November 13, 2024 as a rebrand/upgrade.
- **10,000+ signups within 2 days**; hundreds of thousands within weeks.
- Product Hunt launch (Nov 14, 2024): 366 upvotes, 58 comments.
- Hacker News "Show HN" thread generated strong early discussion.

### The Pivot Story
- Started in 2021 as **Exafunction** (GPU optimization for AI workloads).
- Pivoted in 2022 to Codeium (generative AI coding tools).
- Raised $150M Series C at $1.25B in August 2024.
- Launched Windsurf in November 2024; rebranded fully in April 2025.
- Acquired by OpenAI for $3B in May 2025 (deal later fell through; CEO went to Google).

### Distribution Channels
- **Existing Codeium user base** (800K+): Primary distribution advantage.
- **Enterprise partnerships**: JP Morgan Chase, Zillow, Dell, Anduril, Broadcom.
- **Product Hunt & Hacker News**: Standard developer tool launches.
- **Price competition content**: "Windsurf vs. Cursor" comparison articles became an organic SEO channel.
- **VS Code Marketplace**: Extension available for those who didn't want a new editor.

### What Made People Share It (Viral Mechanics)
1. **"Cursor but cheaper"**: The simplest viral message. $15/mo vs. $20/mo.
2. **Generous free tier**: 25 monthly prompt credits (up from initial 5). Positioned as the most generous free tier in the category.
3. **Cascade "agentic" positioning**: First to market with "agentic IDE" framing, differentiating from Cursor's copilot approach.
4. **Speed**: Users consistently noted Windsurf felt "snappier" than Cursor, generating organic comparison content.

### Pricing Strategy & Its Role in Adoption
- **Free tier**: 25 prompt credits/month (equivalent to ~100 prompts with premium models).
- **Pro**: $15/month (25% cheaper than Cursor at every tier).
- **Pro Ultimate**: $60/month for power users and large teams.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing with SOC2 Type 2, zero-data retention, FedRAMP High.
- **Strategy**: Explicitly undercut Cursor on price to attract cost-sensitive developers (students, junior devs, freelancers).

### Key Insight
Windsurf's growth was built on **existing distribution** (800K Codeium users) and **price competition**. They didn't need to find first users; they needed to convert existing ones. The "agentic IDE" positioning gave them a differentiated narrative, but the real growth lever was being the cheaper, faster alternative to the market leader.

## 4. v0 (Vercel)

### The 30-Second Pitch
"Describe what you want to build in plain English, and v0 generates a working UI with real React components in seconds. Copy, paste, ship."

### First 1,000 Users
- Launched in beta **October 2023** with a waitlist strategy.
- **100,000 waitlist signups in the first 3 weeks.**
- Alpha rolled out to select users; beta expanded to 5,000 additional users.
- First users came from the **existing Vercel/Next.js developer ecosystem** -- v0 was announced at Next.js Conf, tapping into an already-captive audience.
- Product Hunt Runner-up 2023.

### Growth Trajectory
| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Beta launch | October 2023 |
| 100K waitlist | ~3 weeks post-announce |
| v0 for iOS (#1 Product of Day) | 2025 |
| 3M+ users, 1.3M confirmed active | 2025 |
| 100M+ applications generated | Under 1 year |
| ~$42M ARR (est. 21% of Vercel revenue) | February 2025 |
| v0.app full launch (anyone can build) | August 2025 |

### Distribution Channels
- **Vercel/Next.js ecosystem**: Built-in audience of millions of frontend developers.
- **Twitter/X**: Primary viral channel. Developers shared screenshots of AI-generated UIs.
- **Next.js Conf**: Captive audience launch event.
- **Product Hunt**: Runner-up 2023; multiple launches in 2025.
- **"Free0" campaign on X/Twitter**: Offered $100 in credits to first 1,000 people who shared their projects publicly. Direct incentive for viral creation.
- **ChatGPT referrals**: By 2025, ChatGPT drove 10% of Vercel's new signups (up from 1% six months prior). AI search became a major acquisition channel.
- **Community templates**: Shareable, remixable designs created by users became organic growth content.

### What Made People Share It (Viral Mechanics)
1. **Visual output is inherently shareable**: Unlike code (text), v0 generates visible UIs. Screenshots look impressive and travel well on social media.
2. **"I built this in 30 seconds"**: The speed of generation was the viral hook. Prompt-to-UI screenshots were irresistible to share.
3. **Waitlist FOMO**: 100K waitlist in 3 weeks. Scarcity created urgency and social proof.
4. **Community templates**: Users shared designs others could fork, creating a network effect.
5. **Non-developer accessibility**: Marketers, designers, and PMs could use it, expanding the sharing audience beyond developers.

### Pricing Strategy & Its Role in Adoption
- **Separate $20/month premium tier**: Supplements the core Vercel subscription.
- **Dual revenue model**: v0 subscription fees + increased downstream Vercel infrastructure usage (apps built on v0 are deployed on Vercel).
- **Lean team**: ~10 people operating as an independent unit within Vercel.
- **Flywheel**: v0 doubled Vercel's entire user base. More v0 users = more Vercel hosting customers.

### Key Insight
v0 demonstrates the power of **ecosystem leverage**. Vercel didn't need to find new users; they activated their existing Next.js community. The visual, shareable output created natural virality that text-based coding tools can't match. And the $20/mo tier creates a funnel into Vercel's core hosting business -- v0 is both product and distribution channel.

## 5. Claude Code (Anthropic)

### The 30-Second Pitch
"An AI coding agent that lives in your terminal. Tell it what to build, and it reads your files, writes code, runs commands, and commits to git -- all autonomously."

### First 1,000 Users
- Released as a **research preview on February 24, 2025**. No launch event. No viral demo.
- First users were developers already in the Anthropic/Claude ecosystem who discovered it through API docs and developer channels.
- Developer enthusiasm was "real and immediate inside that bubble, with people sharing workflows and building extensions."
- The tool was initially invisible to mainstream audiences: "a developer tool in terminal with a blinking cursor was not accessible to them."

### Growth Trajectory
| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Research preview launch | February 24, 2025 |
| General availability | May 2025 |
| Competitors copy the approach (Codex CLI, Gemini CLI) | April-June 2025 |
| $500M+ annualized revenue | September 2025 |
| Claude Code on the web | October 2025 |
| $1B annualized revenue | November 2025 (~9 months post-launch) |
| ~$2B annualized revenue (est.) | January 2026 |

### Distribution Channels
- **No traditional marketing at launch.** Quiet research preview.
- **Developer word-of-mouth**: Hacker News discussions ("What makes Claude Code so damn good" -- 469 points, 304 comments), Reddit (r/ClaudeCode: 4,200+ weekly contributors).
- **CLAUDE.md configuration sharing**: Developers shared their custom setups, creating organic tutorials and community content.
- **Model quality as marketing**: Claude 3.5/3.7 Sonnet and Claude 4 were consistently rated top coding models, creating pull toward Claude Code.
- **Anthropic blog**: Technical posts showing internal use (marketing team using Claude Code, saving 100+ hrs/month).
- **$100M Claude Partner Network** (2026): Funding consulting firms and integrators to drive enterprise adoption.
- **Hiring a dedicated "Campaign Marketing Manager, Claude Code"**: Signals shift toward more active marketing in 2026.

### What Made People Share It (Viral Mechanics)
1. **Terminal-first differentiation**: In a sea of IDE-based tools, the CLI approach stood out. It felt novel and powerful to developers who live in the terminal.
2. **CLAUDE.md virality**: The configuration file became a meme/cultural artifact. "The creator of Claude Code's Claude setup" was a popular HN thread.
3. **Capability demonstrations**: SWE-bench solve rate of 77.2% with Sonnet 4.5 was a concrete, shareable metric.
4. **"Not just for developers"**: Stories of people using Claude Code for taxes, knitting patterns, and growing tomato plants created novelty-driven sharing.
5. **Anthropic's reputation**: Claude's "thoughtful AI" brand attracted developers who valued quality over hype.

### Pricing Strategy & Its Role in Adoption
- **Pro**: $20/month (~40-80 hours weekly usage)
- **Max**: $100/month (primary daily-user tier)
- **Max+**: $200/month (heavy users)
- **Team**: $25/user/month standard, $150/user/month premium seats
- **Strategy**: Bundled with Claude subscription (not a separate product). This lowered the barrier -- existing Claude users could try it immediately.
- **Contention point**: Weekly usage caps on Max plans triggered significant backlash (the "Claude Is Dead" thread, 841 upvotes, Sept 2025). Rate limits remain the #1 community complaint.

### Key Insight
Claude Code's story is **"The Revolution Nobody Noticed."** It launched with zero fanfare, grew entirely through developer word-of-mouth and model quality, and reached $1B ARR in ~9 months. The lesson: if your underlying model is the best at coding, you don't need marketing -- developers will find you. But the quiet launch also meant ceding narrative control to competitors who launched louder.

## 6. Aider

### The 30-Second Pitch
"AI pair programming in your terminal. Talk to an LLM, and it edits your code directly with proper git commits. Works with any model -- GPT-4, Claude, DeepSeek, or local models via Ollama."

### First 1,000 Users
- Created by **Paul Gauthier** as a solo open-source project.
- Growth was entirely organic: Hacker News posts, GitHub discoverability, and developer word-of-mouth.
- Key HN comments became testimonials: "THANK YOU for Aider! It really feels like a glimpse into the future of coding" (derwiki). "Aider ... is the tool to benchmark against" (BeetleB).
- No company, no funding, no marketing budget. Pure open-source community building.

### Growth Trajectory
| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Initial release | 2023 |
| SOTA SWE-bench Lite score (26.3%) | Mid-2024 |
| 20K GitHub stars | 2024 |
| 41.6K GitHub stars | Early 2026 |

### Distribution Channels
- **Hacker News**: Primary discovery channel. Multiple front-page posts over time. Show HN posts.
- **GitHub**: Stars, issues, and PRs created organic visibility. Active issue resolution built trust.
- **SWE-bench benchmarks**: Publishing competitive benchmark results on the leaderboard drew attention from the AI/dev community. This was a form of marketing-through-credibility.
- **Blog posts (aider.chat/blog)**: Technical deep-dives on the Architect/Editor approach and model comparisons drove organic SEO traffic.
- **Word-of-mouth in terminal-first developer community**: Developers who prefer CLI tools are a tight community that shares recommendations.
- **Model-agnostic positioning**: Supporting every major LLM (and local models) meant every model community could adopt Aider.

### What Made People Share It (Viral Mechanics)
1. **Benchmark results**: Beating commercial tools on SWE-bench was a concrete, shareable achievement for an open-source project.
2. **Model-agnostic flexibility**: Users could share "I use Aider + [their preferred model]" stories, each reaching a different audience.
3. **Git integration**: Auto-committing changes with proper messages was a "wow" feature that felt production-ready.
4. **Open source ethos**: Developers champion projects they believe should exist. Aider as "the free alternative" to Cursor/Copilot had ideological appeal.
5. **Active maintainer**: Paul Gauthier's responsiveness to issues and frequent updates created a sense of momentum.

### Pricing Strategy & Its Role in Adoption
- **Free and open source.** Users pay only for LLM API costs.
- This is the anti-SaaS model: no subscription, no vendor lock-in.
- Attracts developers who are ideologically opposed to paying for an IDE or who want full control over their stack.
- Revenue model: None (as of research date). Aider is a passion project / open-source contribution.

### Key Insight
Aider proves that **benchmark-driven credibility + consistent open-source maintenance + Hacker News visibility** can build a 40K+ star project with zero marketing. The key growth lever was being model-agnostic -- every time a new model launches (GPT-4, Claude 3.5, DeepSeek), Aider gets a fresh wave of attention as people test the new model with it.

## 7. Continue.dev

### The 30-Second Pitch
"The open-source AI coding assistant that works inside your existing IDE. Connect any model, add your own context (Jira, Confluence, codebase), and keep your data private -- all for free."

### First 1,000 Users
- Founded June 2023 by **Ty Dunn** (CEO) and **Nate Sesti** (CTO).
- Went through **Y Combinator S23**. The YC launch ("Launch YC: Continue") provided initial visibility.
- Within 3 months of launch: 5K+ GitHub stars, 750+ Discord members, 30K+ VS Code Marketplace downloads.
- Early growth driven by YC network, Hacker News ("Launch HN: Continue (YC S23)"), and developer communities seeking open-source Copilot alternatives.

### Growth Trajectory
| Milestone | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|
| Founded | June 2023 |
| YC S23 graduation | Late 2023 |
| $2.1M funding (post-YC) | Late 2023 |
| 5K GitHub stars, 30K downloads | ~3 months post-launch |
| $3M seed (Heavybit) | February 2025 |
| 23K+ GitHub stars, 11K Discord | 2025 |
| Hundreds of thousands of users | 2025 |
| Version 1.0 launch | 2025 |

### Distribution Channels
- **Y Combinator network**: YC launch, demo day, and alumni network provided initial distribution.
- **VS Code Marketplace**: Primary discovery channel for developers searching for AI extensions.
- **Ollama ecosystem**: Deep integration with Ollama made Continue the go-to recommendation for local AI coding. Every Ollama blog post and tutorial mentions Continue.
- **Hacker News**: "Launch HN" post and ongoing community discussions.
- **Privacy-focused communities**: Continue's local/self-hosted model support attracted privacy-conscious developers and enterprises.
- **Angel investor networks**: Julien Chaumond (Hugging Face co-founder) and other AI leaders as angels amplified visibility.
- **Partnership content**: Ollama blog post "An entirely open-source AI code assistant inside your editor" was a key referral source.

### What Made People Share It (Viral Mechanics)
1. **"Open-source Copilot" positioning**: Simple, compelling narrative for developers skeptical of Microsoft/GitHub lock-in.
2. **Privacy story**: "Keep your code on your machine" resonated especially with enterprise developers and privacy advocates.
3. **Model flexibility**: Like Aider, supporting any model meant benefiting from every new model release cycle.
4. **IDE integration**: Working inside VS Code and JetBrains (not requiring a new editor) reduced switching costs to near zero.
5. **Customizability**: The ability to add custom context (Jira, Confluence) gave enterprise users something to champion internally.

### Pricing Strategy & Its Role in Adoption
- **Core product**: Free and open source.
- **Commercial model** (evolving): Focus on enterprise features (source-controlled AI checks, CI enforcement).
- **Revenue strategy**: Transitioning from pure open-source to a commercial offering with "quality control for your software factory" positioning -- AI checks on every PR.
- Modest funding ($5.1M total through Feb 2025) signals lean operations.

### Key Insight
Continue.dev grew by **being the open-source default in the right ecosystems**. The Ollama partnership was particularly effective -- as local model usage exploded, Continue was always the recommended IDE integration. The YC network provided the initial spark, and the "open-source Copilot" narrative provided the ongoing fuel. Their pivot toward enterprise CI/CD quality checks (rather than just code completion) is a differentiation play for monetization.

## 8. Cross-Cutting Patterns & Best Practices for 2025-2026

### Pattern 1: Product IS the Marketing
Every successful tool spent $0 or near-$0 on traditional marketing. The product experience created the distribution:
- **Cursor**: $0 marketing spend to $1.2B ARR
- **Claude Code**: No launch event, $1B ARR in 9 months
- **Aider**: Solo developer, 41K GitHub stars

Developer tools that need to be "marketed" are probably not good enough.

### Pattern 2: The "Wow Moment" Must Be Shareable
The fastest-growing tools all created a moment that demanded sharing:
- **Cursor**: "I typed English and code appeared" --> tweet
- **v0**: "I described a UI and got a working React component" --> screenshot
- **Devin**: "It autonomously fixed a bug" --> video
- **Aider**: "It edited my files and committed to git" --> HN comment

**Visual output travels faster** (v0's screenshots > Cursor's text > Claude Code's terminal).

### Pattern 3: Generous Free Tiers Create Evangelists
Conversion rates correlate with free tier generosity:
- **Cursor**: 2,000 free completions/month. 36% conversion rate.
- **Windsurf**: 25 free prompt credits/month. Positioned as "most generous."
- **Aider/Continue**: Completely free (open source).

The free tier is not a funnel; it's an army of evangelists. Make it genuinely useful.

### Pattern 4: Ride the Model Release Cycle
Every major model release creates a marketing event for tools that support it:
- New Claude model --> Claude Code and Aider get attention
- New GPT model --> Every tool gets comparison content
- New open-source model (DeepSeek, Qwen) --> Aider and Continue benefit

**Model-agnostic tools get free marketing every few weeks.**

### Pattern 5: Hacker News + Twitter/X = Developer Distribution
The two channels that matter most:
- **Hacker News**: Credibility, deep technical discussion, long-tail SEO. A front-page HN post is worth more than any ad campaign.
- **Twitter/X**: Viral moments, screenshots, hot takes. This is where "vibe coding" and tool comparisons happen.

Secondary channels: Product Hunt (good for launch day, less for sustained growth), YouTube (long-form tutorials), Reddit (community building), Discord (retention and support).

### Pattern 6: Fork, Don't Build From Scratch
- **Cursor**: Forked VS Code. Instant ecosystem compatibility.
- **Windsurf**: Also VS Code-based.
- **Continue**: Extension for VS Code and JetBrains.

Building a new IDE from scratch is a death sentence. Meet developers where they already are.

### Pattern 7: Controversy Is Awareness
- **Devin**: Debunking videos generated more awareness than the original demo.
- **Cursor**: "Is AI replacing developers?" articles drove tool discovery.
- **Claude Code**: Rate limit complaints kept it in discussion.

In AI, the worst thing is being ignored. Even negative attention converts.

### Pattern 8: Enterprise Follows Bottom-Up Adoption
Every tool followed the same sequence:
1. Individual developer discovers and adopts
2. Developer brings it to their team
3. Team adoption triggers enterprise procurement
4. Enterprise features (SSO, compliance, SOC2) get built

**Cursor**: Individual --> team --> 50,000+ enterprise accounts, half the Fortune 500.
**Windsurf**: Developer extension --> enterprise contracts at Dell, JP Morgan, Zillow.

Top-down enterprise sales without bottom-up adoption does not work for coding tools.

### Pattern 9: Pricing is Converging on $20/month
The market has settled on a standard:
| Tool | Free Tier | Pro Price |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Cursor | 2,000 completions | $20/mo |
| Claude Code | Bundled with Claude Pro | $20/mo |
| Windsurf | 25 prompt credits | $15/mo |
| Devin 2.0 | - | $20/mo + pay-per-use |
| v0 | Limited | $20/mo |
| GitHub Copilot | - | $19/mo |

$20/month is the "impulse buy" threshold for developers. Below it, they don't need approval. Above it, friction increases dramatically.

### Pattern 10: The Terminal Is Back
Claude Code's success validated CLI-based tools:
- **Claude Code**: Terminal-first, $1B ARR
- **Aider**: Terminal-based, 41K stars
- **OpenAI Codex CLI**: Followed Claude Code's approach
- **Google Gemini CLI**: Same

IDE-based tools (Cursor, Windsurf, Copilot) still dominate market share, but the terminal is a viable, differentiated distribution channel -- especially for power users.

## The 2025-2026 Playbook for Launching a Developer Tool

Based on the above research, here is the current best-practice playbook:

### Pre-Launch
1. **Build on an existing ecosystem** (VS Code, terminal, existing IDE). Do not ask developers to learn something new.
2. **Create a genuinely generous free tier.** 2,000+ actions/month or fully open source.
3. **Identify your "wow moment"** -- the 30-second experience that makes someone say "I have to share this."
4. **Build a small beta community** (Discord, 50-200 engaged early users) who feel like co-creators.

### Launch
5. **Post to Hacker News** (Show HN) and **Twitter/X** simultaneously. These are the two channels.
6. **Launch on Product Hunt** for the badge and initial visibility (but don't over-index on it).
7. **Have 3-5 influential developers** (not paid influencers -- real users) share authentic experiences on launch day.
8. **Publish a benchmark or concrete metric** (SWE-bench score, productivity gain, lines of code generated). Give people something to cite.

### Post-Launch
9. **Respond to every HN comment and GitHub issue.** Treat early users as collaborators, not customers.
10. **Ship weekly.** Visible iteration creates community momentum and recurring content.
11. **Be model-agnostic** if possible. Every new model release becomes free marketing.
12. **Let controversy happen.** Don't suppress debates about your tool. Engage thoughtfully. Attention is the scarcest resource.
13. **Track PQLs (Product Qualified Leads)**, not MQLs. Users who hit usage thresholds are your sales pipeline.
14. **Layer enterprise sales on top of PLG** only after significant bottom-up adoption. Never before.

### What NOT to Do
- Do not spend on paid ads. Developer tools with paid ads signal weak products.
- Do not gate basic features behind payment. Developers will choose the free alternative.
- Do not launch with enterprise-only pricing (Devin's $500/mo mistake).
- Do not build a new editor from scratch (learn from everyone who forked VS Code).
- Do not overpromise in demos (learn from Devin's backlash).
